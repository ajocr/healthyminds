Major Depressive Disorder

Commonly termed as depression, this is a common yet grave mood disorder that should not take lightly. It negatively affects people in their day-to-day activities as it feels like one is continuously carrying a heavy burden. According to the World Health Organisation, more than 264 million people of all ages suffer from this disorder. While this is more common amongst women, teens and young adults, please note that anyone is susceptible to the illness and hence, you are not alone!

As stated earlier, MDD usually poses as a daily challenge to those who have it. These challenges can become so unbearable that people choose to end their lives to end their suffering.

However, everybody is different.

The intensity of the health condition on each person varies. Hence, it is paramount that one is aware and professionally diagnosed for treatment to begin as soon as possible.

Types of MDD

Yes, there are types of depression, and you must be in the know. Each type has its set of symptoms; however, both can be chronic and liable to terrible relapses, especially when untreated.

Even though you will be knowledgeable in a second, self-diagnosis is not advised as it can lead to more dangerous outcomes.

Now to the types,

Recurrent Depressive Disorder

  • Involves recurring depressive episodes only
  • Likely to suffer from anxiety symptoms (see page)
  • The feeling of low self-esteem and guilt
  • Loss of appetite
  • Troubled sleep

Bipolar Affective Disorder

  • Alternates between depressive episodes and manic episodes
  • Usually separated by the normal mood
  • The normal mood is largest subjective as everyone is different.

Prevention Measures

Depression stems from psychological, social, biological factors. However, there are programs and general activities that can help reduce depression.

Some include:

  1. Mental health awareness programs in schools and at workplaces
  2. Interventions for people exhibiting potential symptoms of depression
  3. Exercises to improve positive thinking
Generally,
  1. Have and eat a healthy diet
  2. Get enough sleep
  3. Engage in self-care activities like meditation and yoga (check out our yoga page!)
