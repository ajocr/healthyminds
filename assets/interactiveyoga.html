Yoga Poses

Child's Pose

    Difficulty: 1 out of 5

    The child's pose is a great pose to do in between difficult poses. It relaxes the body
    while stretching the thighs, hips, and ankles.

Downward Facing Dog Pose

    Difficulty: 2 out of 5

    The downward facing dog pose is a great pose for calming the mind. It stimulates blood circulation and rests
    the spine. This pose also strengthens the entire body and stretches the back side of the body.

Warrior I Pose

    Difficulty: 2 out of 5

    Something great about this pose is that it energizes the entire body. It also improves balance, stability, and focus.

Warrior II Pose

    Difficulty: 2 out of 5

    The warrior II pose is beneficial because it stretches the hips, groin, and shoulders.
    It also improves circulation and helps develop balance.

Upward facing dog Pose

    Difficulty: 3 out of 5

    This pose is known for improving lung capacity. This is because while holding the pose, the chest is
    stretched greatly, allowing a lot of room to enter and stay in the lungs.

Triangle Pose

    Difficulty: 3 out of 5

    The triangle pose improves digestion and constipation, as well as stretches and strengthens the
    muscles in the legs. This pose also relieves stress.

Tree Pose

    Difficulty: 3 out of 5

    The tree pose helps with balance and stability in the legs. It also strengthens the ligaments
    in the foot, and tones all the muscles up the leg to the butt.

Low Lunge Pose

    Difficulty: 4 out of 5

    While doing the low lunge, tension in the hips will be released. This pose also strengthens the knees and
    stretches the hamstrings, quads, and groin. Additionally, this pose helps build mental focus.

Bridge Pose

    Difficulty: 4 out of 5

    This pose strengthens the muscles in the back, butt, legs, and ankles. It is also great for opening up the
    chest and rejuvenating tired legs

Plank Pose

    Difficulty: 5 out of 5

    The plank channels and tones all of the muscles in the core. While strengthening the
    core muscles, this pose also improves posture if done correctly.
